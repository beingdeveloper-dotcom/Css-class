All pseudo class and Combinators

  1. adjacent Sibling Combinator

    2. The adjacent sibilings combinator(+)separates two selectors and matches the second element only if it immediately follows the first element, and both are chilldren of the same parent element

    Example:

    I am paragraph one

    I am paragraph tow

    I am sub adjacent one

    I am sub adjacent two

  2. General sibling combinator

    3. The General sibilings combinator(~)separates two selectors and matches all iterations of the second element, that are following the first element (though not necessarily immediately), and are children of the same parent

    Example:

    I am pargraph one

    I am pargraph two

    I am pargraph three

    I am pargraph foue

    I am pargraph five

    I am pargraph six

  3. only-of-type:

    The :only-of-type CSS pseudo class represents an element that has no sibilings of the same type.

    Example:
    I am `div` #1.

    I am the only `p` among my siblings.

    I am `div` #2.
    I am `div` #3. I am the only `i` child. I am `em` #1. I am `em` #2.

  4. :first-of-type

    The :first-of-type CSS pseudo class represents the first element of its type among a group of sibiling elements

    Example 1:

    heading one

    paragraph one

    paragraph two

    paragraph three

    Example 2:

    heading one

    heading two

    heading three

    paragraph one

    paragraph two

    span 1

    paragraph three


  5. :last-of-type

    The :last-of-type CSS pseudo class represents the last element of its type among a group of sibilings

    Example:

    heading one

    heading two

    heading three

    paragraph one

    paragraph two

    span 1

    paragraph three


  6. :not

    7. The :not() CSS pseudo class represents element that do match a list of selectors.Since it prevents specific items from being selected, it is known as the negation pseudo-class.

    The :not() pseudo-class requires a comma-separated list of one or more selectors as its argument. The list must not contain another negation selector or a pseudo-element.

    Example:

    I am a paragraph.

    I am so very fancy!

    I am NOT a paragraph.

    foo inside h2 bar inside h2

  7. :out-of-range

    The :out-of-rangeCSS pseudo class represents an "input" element whose current value is outside the range limits specified by the "min" and "max" attributes.

    Example:

    Values between 1 and 10 are valid.

  8. :in-range

    9. The :in-range CSS pseudo-class represents an "input"element whose current value is within the range limits specified by the "min" and "max" attributes.
